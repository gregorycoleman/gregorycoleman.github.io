Building Software Is A Craft.

Be A Craftsman.

I build projects for clients. I like building great products and I consider it my craft. The balancing of features, time, needs, budget and the unforeseen while still making your client happy it what consulting is all about. To build great products, you have to do all of the above, and go one step further and connect with your users.

I was once turned down for a job because I was too "client facing". This was surprising because I think everyone should be client facing. No one wants a DevOps guy in their office who doesn't care about the client. No one wants a UX developer who doesn't care about the user. A good developer understands that code is just a means to an end -- and that end is making someone's life better.

As software developers and DevOps Engineers, we are all client facing and we build products to help. We build software to help make stakeholder's lives easier, better or just more fun. We solve problems and generally do good stuff.

First, do no harm. An important step for medical students is the Hippocratic Oath. For developers, it should be, First, do something awesome.
